# skykernel

> **Keep your tools. Change your cloud.**

A surprising amount of the modern cloud is really one cloud's shape pressed into everything around it: the SDKs, the command-line habits, the service names, the mental model your whole team carries. That shape is genuinely good — which is exactly why being locked to a single provider by it is such an expensive trap. The work here begins from a simple wish: keep the familiar, provider-shaped tooling, but let what runs underneath it be anything.

The idea is a thin, faithful surface that speaks the dialect applications already know, sitting on a provider-neutral core that can be backed by one substrate today and a different one tomorrow. Storage, queues, messaging, compute, functions, containers — the everyday primitives — exposed through an interface your existing tools and instincts already fit, with the choice of where they actually run pushed down out of the application's sight.

The promise is portability without a rewrite and without relearning. Build against the shape you know; decide later, and change your mind freely, about whose hardware it lands on.
